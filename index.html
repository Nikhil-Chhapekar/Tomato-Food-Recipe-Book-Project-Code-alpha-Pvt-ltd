Maggi

About Meal

Maggi is a popular brand of instant noodles known for its quick and easy preparation. Made from wheat flour and water, Maggi noodles are often accompanied by a flavor packet containing spices and herbs. Widely enjoyed as a quick snack or light meal, Maggi is customizable with added vegetables or proteins. The brand has gained a global following, especially in Asian countries, and has become a cultural icon due to its convenience and unique taste.

Ingridients:


1 packet Maggi noodles
1 cup water
1 tablespoon oil
1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
1 medium-sized tomato, finely chopped
1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.)
1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste (optional)
1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust according to your spice preference)
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
Salt to taste
Chopped coriander leaves for garnishing
Lemon wedges for serving

Instruction:


Noodles Preparation: Boil 1 cup of water in a pan. Once it starts boiling, add the Maggi noodles and cook until they are soft. Drain the water and keep the noodles aside.
Vegetable Preparation: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste (if using) and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes until they become slightly tender. Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
Seasoning: Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2 minutes.
Combine Noodles and Vegetables: Add the boiled Maggi noodles to the vegetable mixture. Mix everything well, ensuring the noodles are coated with the spices.
Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot, optionally with lemon wedges on the side.
In a wide pan, melt the butter or margarine. Add the onions. Cook until onions are soft.
Put-in the ginger and cook for 2 minutes
Add the chicken liver. Crush the chicken liver while cooking it in the pan.
Add the chopped pig ears and pork belly. Cook for 10 to 12 minutes
Put-in the soy sauce, garlic powder, and chili. Mix well
Add salt and pepper to taste
Put-in the mayonnaise and mix with the other ingredients
Transfer to a serving plate. Top with chopped green onions and raw egg.
Serve hot. Share and Enjoy (add the lemon or calamansi before eating)

Poha

About Meal

Poha is a popular Indian breakfast dish made with flattened rice (poha), sautéed with a mix of vegetables, spices, and peanuts. It's a quick and light dish, often garnished with fresh coriander and served with lemon wedges. Poha is a flavorful and nutritious choice for breakfast or a light meal.

Ingridients:

1 cup thick poha (flattened rice)
1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
1 medium-sized potato, peeled and diced
1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
8-10 curry leaves
1/4 cup peanuts
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil
Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
Lemon wedges for serving

Instruction:

1. Rinse the poha under running water for a minute. Drain excess water and set it aside. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes to soften.
2. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
Add peanuts and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add curry leaves, chopped green chilies, and onions. Sauté until onions become translucent.
Add diced potatoes and cook until they are slightly tender. If needed, sprinkle some water and cover the pan to cook the potatoes faster.
Add green peas and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
3. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the soaked and softened poha to the pan.
Add turmeric powder and salt. Mix everything gently to combine. Cook for an additional 3-5 minutes on low heat.
4. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
5. Serve hot with a side of lemon wedges. Poha is often enjoyed with sev, grated coconut, or a dollop of yogurt.

Chiken Biryani

About Meal

Marinate chicken in yogurt and spices. Cook aromatic basmati rice separately. Sauté onions, add tomatoes, and blend with marinated chicken. Layer rice and chicken, top with ghee, fried onions, and saffron milk. Dum cook for 20-25 minutes. Fluff and serve hot. Enjoy the flavorful and aromatic Chicken Biryani!

Ingridients:

For Marinating Chicken:
- 500g chicken, cut into pieces
- 1 cup yogurt
- 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- Salt to taste
For Rice:
- 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
- 4-5 cups water
- 1 bay leaf
- 4-5 green cardamom pods
- 4-5 cloves
- 1-inch cinnamon stick
- Salt to taste
For Biryani Masala:
- 2 large onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
- 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
- 1 teaspoon biryani masala powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 cup fried onions (for garnish)
For Layering:
- Ghee or oil
- Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for color and aroma)

Instruction:

  • Marinate the Chicken:
    • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 cup yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
  • Cook the Rice:
    • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
    • 4-5 cups water
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 4-5 green cardamom pods
    • 4-5 cloves
    • 1-inch cinnamon stick
    • Salt to taste
  • Prepare Biryani Masala:
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
    • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon biryani masala powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  • Layering:
  • Ghee or oil
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for color and aroma)
  • Dum Cooking:
  • Cover the pan tightly with a lid or aluminum foil.
  • Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to cook completely.
  • Serve Hot:
  • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing the layers.
  • Serve hot with raita or a side salad.

    Omelette

    About Meal

    Whisk eggs, season with salt and pepper. Cook in a hot, greased pan until set. Add desired fillings like cheese, vegetables, or ham. Fold and serve for a quick, delicious, and customizable breakfast or brunch option.

    Ingridients:


    3 eggs, beaten
    1 tsp sunflower oil
    1 tsp butter

    Instruction:


    1. Beat the eggs: Use two or three eggs per omelette, depending on how hungry you are. Beat the eggs lightly with a fork. 2. Melt the butter: Use an 8-inch nonstick skillet for a 2-egg omelette, a 9-inch skillet for 3 eggs. Melt the butter over medium-low heat, and keep the temperature low and slow when cooking the eggs so the bottom doesn’t get too brown or overcooked. 3. Add the eggs: Let the eggs sit for a minute, then use a heatproof silicone spatula to gently lift the cooked eggs from the edges of the pan. Tilt the pan to allow the uncooked eggs to flow to the edge of the pan. 4. Fill the omelette: Add the filling—but don’t overstuff the omelette—when the eggs begin to set. Cook for a few more seconds 5. Fold and serve: Fold the omelette in half. Slide it onto a plate with the help of a silicone spatula.

    Semolina(Upma)

    About Meal

    Roasted semolina cooked with a tempering of mustard seeds, urad dal, and cumin, along with sautéed vegetables like onions, carrots, and peas. Seasoned with turmeric and garnished with fresh coriander, Upma is a quick, savory breakfast or brunch dish, offering a warm and comforting start to the day.

    Ingridients:

    1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
    2 tablespoons oil or ghee
    1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    1 teaspoon urad dal (black gram)
    1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
    1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
    1 green chili, finely chopped
    1 onion, finely chopped
    1 carrot, finely chopped
    1/4 cup green peas
    2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
    1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    Salt to taste
    2 cups water
    Lemon wedges (optional, for serving)

    Instruction:

    1. **Roast Semolina:**
    - Heat a pan over medium heat and dry roast the semolina until it turns light brown and gives a nutty aroma. Keep stirring to avoid burning. Once done, remove it from the pan and set aside. 2. **Prepare Tempering:**
    - In the same pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, chana dal, and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter. 3. **Saute Aromatics:**
    - Add chopped ginger and green chili. Saute for a minute until the raw smell disappears. 4. **Add Vegetables:**
    - Add chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent. Add carrots, green peas, and continue to sauté for another 2-3 minutes. 5. **Cook Semolina:**
    - Add the roasted semolina to the pan. Mix well to coat the semolina with the tempered ingredients and vegetables. 6. **Season and Add Water:**
    - Add turmeric powder, salt, and water to the pan. Stir continuously to avoid lumps. Bring it to a boil. 7. **Simmer and Cover:**
    - Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes or until the upma is cooked and the water is absorbed. 8. **Garnish and Serve:**
    - Once cooked, fluff the upma with a fork. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with lemon wedges if desired.

    Lumpia

    About Meal

    Different spring rolls known as "lumpia" are popular in the Philippines and Indonesia. Lumpia are formed of a thin pastry skin, also known as a "lumpia wrapper," that encloses either savory or sweet fillings. It may be either fresh or deep-fried and is frequently served as an appetizer or snack.

    Ingridients:


    50 pieces lumpia wrapper (Up to you how many pieces)
    3 cups cooking oil
    1 1/2 lbs ground pork
    2 pieces onion minced
    2 pieces carrots minced
    1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
    1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
    1/2 cup parsley chopped
    1 1/2 teaspoons salt
    1 tablespoon sesame oil
    2 eggs

    Instruction:


    1. Combine all filling ingredients in a bowl. Mix well.
    2. Scoop around 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons of filling and place over a piece of lumpia wrapper. Spread the filling and then fold both sides of the wrapper. Fold the bottom. Brush beaten egg mixture on the top end of the wrapper. Roll-up until completely wrapped. Perform the same step until all mixture are consumed.
    3. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Deep fry lumpia in medium heat until it floats.
    4. Remove from the pot. Let excess oil drip. Serve. Share and enjoy

    Samosa

    About Meal

    Samosa is a popular Indian snack featuring a crispy, triangular pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes other vegetables. The dough is made from all-purpose flour, while the filling is seasoned with aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala. These flavorful parcels are deep-fried until golden brown, resulting in a delicious, crunchy exterior with a warm and flavorful interior. Samosas are often served with chutney or sauce and are enjoyed as a tasty appetizer or snack.

    Ingridients:

    For the Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
    1/4 cup oil
    Water, as needed
    A pinch of salt
    For the Filling: 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
    1 cup boiled and chopped mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, and green beans work well)
    1 finely chopped onion
    1 teaspoon grated ginger
    1 teaspoon minced garlic
    1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
    1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    1 teaspoon coriander powder
    1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    Salt, to taste
    Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    Oil for frying

    Instruction:

    Prepare the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and oil.
    Gradually add water and knead the mixture to form a smooth and stiff dough.
    Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
    Prepare the Filling: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    Add chopped onions, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until the onions become translucent.
    Add green chilies and stir for a minute.
    Add boiled and mashed potatoes, mixed vegetables, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
    Cook the filling for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    Add chopped coriander leaves, mix, and let the filling cool.
    Shape and Fill the Samosas: Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls.
    Roll each ball into a thin oval or round shape.
    Cut the rolled dough in half to form two semi-circles.
    Create a cone shape by folding each semi-circle and sealing the edges with water.
    Fill the cone with the prepared potato and vegetable filling, ensuring it's not overfilled.
    Seal the open edge of the cone to form a triangular samosa.
    Frying the Samosas: Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat for frying.
    Once the oil is hot, gently slide the prepared samosas into the oil.
    Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.
    Remove the samosas and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.
    Serve: Serve the samosas hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.

    Chicken Burger

    About Meal

    Prepare the buns! For the ideal stuffing for juicy burgers, coat or marinate chicken thighs and breast. Dig it after adding toppings like slaw and sauce along with your selected side dishes.

    Ingridients:


    3 boneless chicken breasts (around half a kg)
    1 tspn of pepper
    1 tspn of salt
    2 tblspns of worcestershire sauce
    1 tspn of Mustard powder or all spice powder
    2 tblspns of Flour
    1 Egg
    1 1/2 cups of Breadcrumbs
    Soft seeded burger buns
    Mayonnaise or KFC Zinger Sauce

    Instruction:


    1. If your chicken breasts are too big, slice them before cooking. Then, marinade the chicken breasts in the Worcestershire sauce, pepper, salt, mustard, or all-spice powder. Leave the chicken for at least four hours or over night.
    2. An egg and two tablespoons of water should be beaten together to make the batter, which should then be set aside.
    3. Chicken breasts that have been marinated should be heavily dusted in flour. They should be well coated after being dipped in both the egg and the breadcrumbs.
    4. The coated chicken breasts should be deep-fried in heated oil over a medium-high heat until they are crisp and golden brown.
    5. The finished burger should be stacked with the lettuce on top, followed by the chicken, and then the mayonnaise or KFC zinger sauce. The buns should be cut in half and lightly toasted. If you'd like a cheesy kick, add some sliced American cheese.

    Manchurian

    About Meal

    Vegetable Manchurian is a delectable Indo-Chinese dish featuring flavorful vegetable balls made from a mix of finely chopped vegetables, coated in a seasoned batter, and deep-fried to golden perfection. The crispy vegetable balls are then tossed in a savory and tangy Manchurian sauce, which includes a blend of soy sauce, chili sauce, and other aromatic ingredients. This dish offers a delightful combination of textures and tastes, making it a popular appetizer or accompaniment to rice or noodles in many Asian cuisines.

    Ingridients:

    For the Vegetable Balls: 1 cup finely chopped mixed vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, etc.)
    1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    1/4 cup cornflour
    1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    Salt to taste
    Black pepper powder to taste
    Soy sauce - 1 teaspoon
    Oil for deep frying
    For the Manchurian Sauce: 1 tablespoon oil
    1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
    1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
    1/4 cup finely chopped spring onions (white part)
    1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers (any color)
    2 tablespoons soy sauce
    1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
    1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
    1 teaspoon vinegar
    1 teaspoon sugar
    1 teaspoon cornflour mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening)
    Salt to taste
    Chopped spring onion greens for garnish

    Instruction:

    For the Vegetable Balls: In a bowl, combine finely chopped mixed vegetables, all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, salt, and black pepper powder.
    Mix well to form a thick batter. If the mixture is too dry, you can add a little water.
    Heat oil in a pan for deep frying. Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into small balls.
    Fry the vegetable balls until they turn golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
    For the Manchurian Sauce: Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped garlic and ginger, sauté for a minute.
    Add chopped spring onions (white part) and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.
    Add soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
    In a small bowl, mix cornflour with water to make a smooth paste. Add this paste to the pan to thicken the sauce. Stir continuously to avoid lumps.
    Once the sauce thickens, add the fried vegetable balls. Toss the balls in the sauce until they are well coated.
    Garnish with chopped spring onion greens.
    Serve hot as an appetizer or with steamed rice/noodles.

    Gulab Jamun

    About Meal

    Gulab Jamun is a beloved Indian dessert renowned for its irresistible sweetness and soft texture. These delectable, round dumplings are made from a combination of milk powder, flour, and ghee, forming a dough that is shaped into small balls and deep-fried to a golden brown hue. After frying, they are soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup infused with cardamom and sometimes saffron, allowing the Gulab Jamuns to absorb the sweetness and become delightfully moist. Often adorned with chopped nuts, these syrup-soaked treats are a cherished indulgence at festive occasions and celebrations.

    Ingridients:

    Ingredients: 1 cup milk powder
    1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
    2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed)
    A pinch of baking soda
    Oil or ghee for frying.
    For the Sugar Syrup: 1 cup sugar
    1/2 cup water
    1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    A few strands of saffron (optional)

    Instruction:


    For the Gulab Jamun: In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, and baking soda.
    Gradually add milk and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough. If needed, add more milk.
    Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into round balls, ensuring they are smooth and crack-free.
    Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Fry the gulab jamun balls until they turn golden brown, then remove and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
    For the Sugar Syrup: In a separate pan, combine sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Bring it to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
    Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using). Simmer the syrup for a few minutes until it slightly thickens.
    Assembling: Allow the fried gulab jamun balls to cool slightly before adding them to the warm sugar syrup.
    Let the gulab jamuns soak in the syrup for at least a few hours to absorb the sweetness.
    Garnish with chopped nuts if desired.

    Rasgulla

    About Meal

    Rasgulla is a popular and delightful Indian dessert known for its spongy texture and sweet taste. Made from fresh cottage cheese (chhena), the cheese balls are gently cooked in a sugar syrup until they absorb the sweetness and become light and airy. Rasgullas are often served chilled, making them a refreshing and beloved treat, especially during festivals and celebrations. This classic dessert is cherished for its simplicity and the burst of sweetness it brings to the palate

    Ingridients:

    1 liter full-fat milk
    2 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar
    1 cup sugar
    4 cups water
    A pinch of cardamom powder (optional)

    Instruction:

    Prepare the Chhena (Cottage Cheese): Boil the milk in a large pot. Add lemon juice or vinegar to the boiling milk to curdle it. Once the milk has curdled, strain the whey using a muslin cloth to obtain the chhena (cottage cheese). Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the lemony flavor. Knead the Chhena: Knead the chhena on a clean surface for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft. Shape into Balls: Divide the chhena into small portions and roll them into smooth, crack-free balls. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a wide pan, mix sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Bring the syrup to a boil and then simmer. Cook the Rasgullas: Gently add the chhena balls to the simmering sugar syrup. Cover the pan and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the rasgullas are soft, spongy, and doubled in size. Cool and Serve: Let the rasgullas cool to room temperature. Optionally, sprinkle a pinch of cardamom powder on top for flavor. Chill (optional): For a refreshing touch, refrigerate the rasgullas before serving.
